Section 1 of 5

GSP+ Origin Rules

Pakistan's zero-tariff benefit only applies if your goods genuinely qualify. These checks ensure your shipment can claim the GSP+ preferential rate — saving your buyer 9–12% in customs duties.

0% complete
⚠️
Most costly mistake: Declaring GSP+ on fabric that was woven outside Pakistan (e.g. imported from China), then only cut and sewn in Pakistan. This fails the double transformation rule. Your buyer will be billed the full tariff and will recover it from you.
💡
Ahmed's Example

Ahmed buys Pakistani yarn, weaves it into fabric at his Faisalabad mill, then cuts and sews the finished linen — both steps in Pakistan. He qualifies for GSP+. If he instead imports Chinese grey fabric and only sews it in Faisalabad, he does NOT qualify.

HS Code & Product Classification
I know the correct HS code for my products Critical
Garments (knitted) = HS Ch. 61 · Garments (woven) = HS Ch. 62 · Home textiles = HS Ch. 63 · Cotton fabric = HS Ch. 52. Wrong HS code = wrong tariff rate.
The HS code on my invoice matches the HS code on my bill of lading
Mismatches between documents trigger automatic customs flags and delays at the EU port of entry.
Double Transformation — Origin Qualification
My fabric was woven or knitted in Pakistan — not imported as ready-made fabric Critical
EU double transformation rule: both weaving AND cutting/sewing must happen in Pakistan. Fabric imported from China or India and only sewn in Pakistan does NOT qualify for GSP+.
I have supplier invoices or mill certificates showing where my yarn and fabric were sourced
If EU customs audits your origin declaration, you need paper evidence. Keep these records for at least 3 years.
REX Registration or Form A Certificate
I am registered as a REX exporter with TDAP — OR — I have a Form A certificate for this shipment Critical
Without one of these, your buyer cannot claim the GSP+ zero tariff at EU customs. REX registration is free via TDAP (tdap.gov.pk) and takes 2–4 weeks. Apply once, use forever.
My commercial invoice includes the correct REX origin declaration statement
The exact required text: "I, the exporter of the products covered by this document, declare that, except where otherwise clearly indicated, these products are of Pakistan preferential origin." Followed by your REX number.
I understand that if my origin declaration is wrong, my buyer will be billed the full tariff — and will recover it from me
On a $20,000 shipment of garments, the full MFN tariff can be $2,000–$2,400. This amount becomes your liability if the origin declaration fails.
Section 2 of 5

REACH Chemical Testing

The EU bans certain chemicals in textile products. If your fabric contains banned substances above legal limits, EU customs can reject and destroy your entire container. A pre-shipment test costs $150–$400. A failed container costs thousands.

0% complete
🚨
If your shipment fails at EU customs: The container is quarantined, then destroyed at your cost. Your buyer loses their sale and claims damages. Your company is entered on the EU RAPEX alert system — visible to all EU importers. A $200 test prevents all of this.
💡
Ahmed's Example

Ahmed sends one white fabric sample and one coloured border fabric sample to SGS Lahore before shipping 200 boxes of bed linen. Cost: $180. Test takes 7 days. Both pass. He attaches the certificates to his shipping documents. The container clears Hamburg customs without issue.

Pre-Shipment Testing
I have sent fabric samples to an accredited lab at least 10 days before my planned ship date Critical
Accredited labs in Pakistan: SGS (+92-42-111-747-474) · Bureau Veritas (+92-21-3241-4571) · Intertek (+92-21-3260-7100). Testing takes 5–10 working days.
I tested every distinct fabric type in this shipment separately
White fabric and coloured/printed fabric must be tested separately. Different dye batches = different test results. Do not assume one fabric type clears all.
What the Test Must Cover
My test certificate covers all 5 key substance groups Verify
Check your certificate covers: (1) Azo dyes / carcinogenic amines — BANNED · (2) Formaldehyde — 75 mg/kg children, 300 mg/kg adults · (3) Heavy metals: Lead, Cadmium, Chromium VI · (4) NPE (Nonylphenol Ethoxylates) — 25 mg/kg limit · (5) Phthalates in plastic accessories.
My test certificate is less than 12 months old for this fabric type and dye batch
An old certificate from a previous order does not cover a new dye batch. If you changed dye supplier or dye formula, retest.
Documentation
My test certificate shows a PASS conclusion — not just test values Critical
The certificate conclusion section should say: "All tested parameters comply with applicable EU REACH restrictions." If it says FAIL or shows values above limits — do NOT ship. Contact your dye supplier immediately.
I have included the test certificate PDF in the document pack sent to my buyer and freight forwarder
EU customs can request this at any time during clearance. Your buyer's customs agent needs it available immediately. Email the PDF before the shipment departs Karachi.
Section 3 of 5

Product Labelling

Every textile product sold in the EU must carry specific label information. Missing or incorrect labels mean your Hungarian buyer cannot legally sell your products at retail — resulting in returns or costly relabelling charges deducted from your payment.

0% complete
🇭🇺
Selling to Hungary specifically: The physical product label must include Hungarian language text for retail sale. English-only labels are one of the most common causes of returns from Hungarian importers. Relabelling typically costs $0.20–$0.50 per unit — on 5,000 units, that is up to $2,500 deducted from your payment.
💡
Turn This Into a Competitive Advantage

Send your Hungarian buyer a label PDF template for approval before you print labels at scale. Ask them to confirm the Hungarian translation. Most Pakistani suppliers don't offer this. Buyers notice — and they remember.

My label shows exact fibre composition as percentages Critical
Must be exact: "100% Cotton" or "60% Cotton, 40% Polyester." Not "60/40 cotton poly." Not rounded figures. Every fibre component must be listed. If the filling and cover of a cushion differ, both must be stated.
My label says "Made in Pakistan" — in full, not "PK" or "Origin: Pakistan" Critical
Country of origin is mandatory for all non-EU imports. The full text "Made in Pakistan" is required. Abbreviations or blank fields are not acceptable.
My label uses standard ISO care symbols — not text-only care instructions
The washing machine symbol, iron symbol, bleach triangle, tumble dry symbol etc. are required. Text instructions alone ("machine wash at 40°C") are not sufficient under EU rules. ISO 3758 symbols must be present.
For Hungary: I have provided a Hungarian-language label template to my buyer for approval Action
Key Hungarian terms: "100% Pamut" (100% Cotton) · "Gyártva: Pakisztán" (Made in Pakistan) · "60°C-on mosható" (machine wash 60°C). A freelance translator on Fiverr translates a label for $10–$20. Do this once, reuse the template.
My label template has been approved by my buyer before large-scale label printing
Send a PDF of the exact label design before you print thousands of labels. Catching an error at proof stage costs nothing. Reprinting 10,000 labels after production costs real money and causes delays.
Section 4 of 5

Shipment Documents

Every Pakistan → EU textile shipment requires a specific set of documents. A single missing or mismatched document can hold your container at Hamburg or Rotterdam for days — your buyer misses their sale, and you bear the cost.

0% complete
🚢
Important for Hungary-bound shipments: Hungary is in the EU Customs Union. Your container does not clear customs in Budapest — it clears at the first EU port of entry: Hamburg, Rotterdam, or Koper. Your buyer's customs agent there handles clearance. If documents are wrong, the delay happens in Germany or the Netherlands, not Hungary.
Core Shipping Documents
Commercial invoice is complete: correct HS codes, full company names, actual transaction value Critical
Do not undervalue the invoice to reduce duties — EU customs has price databases and will challenge suspicious valuations. Undervaluation is a legal risk for both you and your buyer. Declare the real price.
Packing list totals match the invoice exactly — same quantities, same HS codes
Invoice: 500 units HS 6302. Packing list: 500 units HS 6302. Bill of lading: 500 units HS 6302. All three must agree. Any mismatch is an automatic customs flag.
Bill of lading or airway bill is issued and shows the correct port of discharge
Sea freight: port of discharge should be Hamburg (Germany), Rotterdam (Netherlands), or Koper (Slovenia) — not "Budapest." Goods travel by truck from the EU port to Hungary after customs clearance.
Compliance Documents
GSP+ origin declaration (REX on invoice) or Form A certificate is included Critical
This is the document that triggers the zero tariff. Without it, your buyer pays full MFN duty (9–12% for garments). This document is your financial responsibility to provide correctly.
REACH test certificate (from SGS, BV, or Intertek) is included in the document pack
The certificate must cover all fabric types in this shipment and be less than 12 months old. Send the PDF to your buyer and freight forwarder before the vessel departs Karachi.
I have cross-checked all documents against each other before sending to the freight forwarder
Final cross-check: do the invoice HS codes, quantities, and unit prices all match the packing list? Does the bill of lading match both? Five minutes of checking here prevents days of customs delays.
Section 5 of 5

Sustainability & Factory Profile

Large EU fashion brands and retailers must report on their supply chains under EU sustainability rules. This means they will ask you — their Pakistani supplier — for information about your factory. Being ready to answer wins contracts.

0% complete
📋
Does the EU CSRD apply to Ahmed's factory directly? No. The EU Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (simplified December 2025) now applies only to companies with more than 1,000 employees and more than €450M turnover. Ahmed's factory is not directly obligated. But Ahmed's EU buyers ARE — and they will ask Ahmed for data.
💡
Ahmed's Tip

Ahmed sends a one-page factory profile with every new buyer enquiry — before they even ask. It includes worker numbers, wages vs minimum wage, his no-child-labour policy, and his SMETA audit date. Buyers who receive this immediately rank him above suppliers who cannot answer basic questions.

Factory Profile Document
I have a one-page factory profile document ready to email to any new buyer Action
Must include: total workers, % women workers, average wage vs Pakistani minimum wage, working hours policy, child labour policy (written), health & safety basics, and any certifications held.
My factory profile includes a written child labour policy and minimum age verification process
EU buyers will not work with suppliers who cannot demonstrate a written policy. A simple one-paragraph statement saying "We do not employ anyone under 18 and verify age at hiring" is sufficient to start.
Social Compliance Certification
I have, or am actively planning, a SMETA social compliance audit
SMETA (Sedex Members Ethical Trade Audit) is the most widely requested audit by EU fashion and retail buyers. Cost: approximately $600–$1,200. Conducted by SGS, Bureau Veritas, or Intertek. Valid 1–2 years. Register on sedexglobal.com.
I keep records of my material inputs — yarn suppliers, fabric suppliers, dye suppliers, countries of origin
The EU Digital Product Passport for textiles is coming (~2027–2028). It will require a QR code on every product linking to material and production data. Start recording this now — building it retroactively is very difficult.
Coming Soon — Digital Product Passport
I am aware the EU Digital Product Passport is coming (~2027–2028) and I am keeping production records now Coming Soon
Not required yet. But factories that can provide full material traceability data when it becomes mandatory will have a significant competitive advantage over those who cannot.
I understand that CSRD does not apply directly to my factory but does affect what my EU buyers will ask me
Large EU retailers (Zara, H&M, Primark, major Hungarian chains) must report on their supply chains. They will pass these questions to you. Having your factory profile ready means you are never caught off guard by a buyer's sustainability questionnaire.
